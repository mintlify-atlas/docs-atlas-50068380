---
title: "Purchasing and supplier management in Dolibarr"
description: "Manage your complete purchasing workflow: supplier catalog, purchase orders, goods reception, and supplier invoice matching and payment."
---

Dolibarr's purchasing workflow mirrors the sales pipeline in reverse: a purchase order goes to a supplier, goods arrive at your warehouse, and a supplier invoice is matched to confirm the expense. Every step is linked, so you always know what has been ordered, what has arrived, and what remains unpaid.

## Supplier records

Suppliers are third-party records with the **Is supplier** flag enabled. You can set this when creating the record or at any time from an existing third-party profile.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a supplier record">
    Go to **Third Parties → New third party**. Enter the company name, then tick **Supplier** under the type checkboxes. Fill in the address, country, and VAT number as needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set supplier-specific pricing">
    On the supplier record, open the **Supplier prices** tab. Link products to this supplier with a supplier reference, unit cost, minimum quantity, and delivery lead time. These prices populate automatically when you add the product to a purchase order.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add supplier contacts">
    Use the **Contacts** tab to store individual contacts at the supplier (account manager, logistics contact, etc.).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
A third party can be both a customer and a supplier simultaneously. Dolibarr keeps sales and purchase documents separate, so there is no risk of confusion between the two sides of the relationship.
</Note>

## Supplier price requests

Before committing to a purchase order, you can request quotes from multiple suppliers for the same product.

- Go to **Purchases → Supplier price requests → New request**.
- Add one or more products and select the suppliers to contact.
- Dolibarr generates a request form PDF that you can email to each supplier.
- As responses come in, record each supplier's quoted price against the request.
- Compare offers side by side, then convert the chosen quote into a purchase order.

## Purchase orders

Navigate to **Purchases → Purchase orders → New purchase order**.

### Building a purchase order

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Supplier** | Select the supplier third-party record. |
| **Order date** | Defaults to today; adjust as needed. |
| **Planned delivery date** | Expected arrival date; used in delivery tracking reports. |
| **Payment terms** | Net 30, on delivery, etc. |
| **Incoterms** | Trade term defining delivery responsibilities (see below). |

Add product lines. If supplier prices are configured, unit costs pre-fill automatically. Adjust quantities and prices as needed.

### Validating and sending

<Steps>
  <Step title="Validate the order">
    Click **Validate** to lock the order and assign it a purchase order number. The order status changes from **Draft** to **Validated**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send to supplier">
    Click **Send by email** to deliver a PDF of the purchase order to your supplier contact. The sent date is recorded on the order.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mark as sent">
    If you sent the order outside Dolibarr (phone, fax), use **Set as sent** to update the status without triggering an email.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reception (goods receipt)

When goods arrive, record a reception against the purchase order.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the purchase order">
    Navigate to the validated purchase order and click **Create reception**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter received quantities">
    For each line, enter the quantity actually received. You can receive less than ordered (partial reception) — the remaining quantity stays open on the order for a future delivery.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign to a warehouse">
    Select the destination warehouse. Stock is increased immediately upon validation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Validate the reception">
    Click **Validate** to post the stock movement. A reception document is created with its own reference number.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Lot and serial number tracking on reception

If the **Product Batches** module is enabled, an extra column appears on the reception form for each product configured with lot/serial tracking:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Lot tracking">
    Enter the supplier's lot number and (optionally) a **best-before date** or **manufacture date**. All units in the reception are assigned to that lot. Dolibarr uses this for FIFO/FEFO picking on outbound movements.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Serial number tracking">
    For products tracked by individual serial number, enter one serial number per unit received. Each serial number is stored as a separate stock entry.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
Enable lot tracking on a product at **Products → Edit product → Batch/Serial tab → Tracking mode**. Changing this setting on a product that already has stock movements is not recommended.
</Tip>

## Supplier invoices

Supplier invoices are created after reception to record the financial obligation.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="From a reception">
    Open the reception record and click **Create supplier invoice**. All lines and amounts carry over. Adjust if the invoice from the supplier differs from the order.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="From a purchase order">
    Open the validated purchase order and click **Create supplier invoice**. Use this path when you invoice before goods arrive (prepayment scenarios).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Manually">
    Go to **Purchases → Supplier invoices → New invoice**. Select the supplier, add lines, and enter the supplier's own invoice number in the **Supplier ref** field.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Matching and payment

1. Validate the supplier invoice. It posts to the **Purchase** accounting journal.
2. Click **Add payment** to record the amount paid, the payment date, method, and bank account used.
3. If you pay multiple supplier invoices in one bank transfer, use **Batch payment** to allocate the total across invoices.

<Warning>
Always enter the **supplier invoice reference** (their document number) in the **Supplier ref** field. This is essential for matching payments and for audit purposes.
</Warning>

## INCOTERMS

INCOTERMS define which party bears transport costs and risk at each stage of delivery.

- Enable INCOTERMS at **Setup → Modules → INCOTERMS**.
- Configure available terms at **Setup → Dictionaries → INCOTERMS** (EXW, FCA, CPT, CIP, DAP, DPU, DDP, FAS, FOB, CFR, CIF are pre-loaded).
- Assign an INCOTERM to a purchase order in the **Conditions** section of the order form. The selected term prints on the purchase order PDF.

## Multi-currency purchasing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable multi-currency">
    Go to **Setup → Modules → Multi-currency** and activate it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the supplier's currency">
    On the supplier record, set the default **currency** under **Commercial information**. This currency pre-fills on new purchase orders for that supplier.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter exchange rate">
    On the purchase order, the current exchange rate is displayed. Adjust it if needed to match the rate on your bank confirmation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Amounts in both currencies">
    The order displays amounts in both the foreign currency and your base currency. The accounting entry uses the base currency equivalent at the rate recorded on the document.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reporting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Supplier order statistics">
    Go to **Purchases → Statistics** to see purchase volumes by supplier, product, or period. Filter by date range to track spending trends.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Pending deliveries">
    **Purchases → Purchase orders** filtered by status **Partially received** or **Validated** shows all orders awaiting full delivery. Use the planned delivery date column to identify overdue orders.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Supplier invoice aging">
    **Purchases → Supplier invoices** filtered to **Unpaid** shows all outstanding payables. Sort by due date to prioritise payment runs.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Product sourcing overview">
    Navigate to **Products → Supplier prices** (backed by `price_suppliers.php`) to see all configured supplier prices for a product side by side, with lead times and minimum quantities.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
