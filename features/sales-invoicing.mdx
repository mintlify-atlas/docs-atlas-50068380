Dolibarr's sales pipeline is a fully connected chain: a single commercial proposal can flow all the way through to a paid invoice without re-entering data at any stage. Each step is a distinct document with its own status lifecycle, giving you a clear audit trail from first contact to final payment.

## Sales lifecycle overview

```text
Commercial Proposal → Customer Order → Invoice → Payment
```

Every transition in the chain is optional — you can skip straight to an invoice if you don't need a proposal or order, or stop at any stage if your workflow requires it.

## Commercial proposals

Navigate to **Commerce → Proposals → New proposal** or create one directly from a third-party record.

### Building a proposal

- Set the **third party** (customer or prospect), the **validity date**, and the **payment terms**.
- Add product or service lines. Each line pulls the product reference, description, unit price, VAT rate, and any customer-specific pricing automatically.
- Apply a **global discount** or a **per-line discount** as a percentage or absolute amount.
- Use the **Notes** tab for internal notes (not printed) and public notes (printed on the PDF).

### Sending and online signing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate the PDF">
    On the validated proposal, click **Generate PDF** and choose a template. Dolibarr renders a formatted proposal PDF using your company logo and details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send by email">
    Click **Send by email** directly from the proposal. The PDF attaches automatically, and the email body can use a configurable template.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share the online signing link">
    Enable the **Online signing** option in proposal settings. Dolibarr generates a unique public URL you can include in the email. The customer opens the link in a browser, reviews the proposal, and clicks **Sign** — no account required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Signature captured">
    Once signed, the proposal status updates to **Signed** and a signed PDF is stored on the record. You receive a notification email.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Converting a proposal

From a validated or signed proposal, use the **Convert to order** or **Create invoice** buttons to advance the pipeline. No data entry is repeated.

<Note>
A proposal can only be converted once. If you need to invoice partial quantities, create the customer order first and then generate invoices from the order.
</Note>

## Customer orders

Access customer orders at **Commerce → Customer orders**.

### Creating an order

- Create from a proposal (recommended) or click **New order** to start from scratch.
- **Stock reservation**: when you validate an order, Dolibarr can automatically reserve stock in the selected warehouse. Enable this at **Setup → Modules → Orders**.

### Generating a shipment

From a validated order, click **Create shipment** to open the shipping form. Set the quantities to ship (for partial shipments), assign a warehouse, and validate the shipment. This updates stock levels immediately.

<Tip>
Use the **Delivery address** feature on the order to ship to an address different from the third party's main address — useful for companies with multiple sites.
</Tip>

## Customer invoices

Navigate to **Billing → Customers → Invoices**.

### Invoice states

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Editable, not yet sent to the customer. VAT is not posted. |
| **Validated** | Locked for editing. Appears in accounting journals. |
| **Partially paid** | At least one payment recorded, balance still outstanding. |
| **Paid** | Fully settled. |
| **Abandoned** | Written off as uncollectable. |

### Creating an invoice

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="From an order">
    Open the customer order and click **Create invoice**. All lines, discounts, and addresses carry over. You can invoice 100% or choose specific lines for partial invoicing.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="From a proposal">
    On a signed or validated proposal that has no order, click **Create invoice** to jump directly to an invoice.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="From scratch">
    Go to **Billing → New invoice**, select the customer, then add lines manually. Use this for one-off invoices or when your workflow doesn't use proposals or orders.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Credit notes

Click **Create credit note** on a validated invoice to open a pre-filled credit note. A credit note reduces the customer's balance and can be linked to a new invoice or refunded as a payment.

### Recurring invoices

Use **Billing → Invoice templates** (backed by `card-rec.php`) to define a recurring invoice:

1. Set the product lines, amounts, and VAT.
2. Choose a recurrence frequency (monthly, quarterly, annually).
3. Set the first generation date. Dolibarr generates draft invoices automatically on schedule.

<Warning>
Recurring invoices generate in **draft** state by default. You must validate and send each one manually unless you configure the auto-validation cron job.
</Warning>

## Recording payments

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the invoice">
    Navigate to the validated invoice. The outstanding balance is shown at the bottom of the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a payment">
    Click **Add payment**. Enter the payment date, amount, payment method (bank transfer, cheque, card, cash), and select the **bank account** the money was received into.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply to multiple invoices (optional)">
    If the customer paid several invoices in one transaction, use **Billing → Batch payment entry** to record a single receipt and allocate it across multiple invoices at once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invoice marked as paid">
    Once the full balance is settled, the invoice status automatically changes to **Paid** and the payment is posted to the accounting journal.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## End-to-end "create and close a sale" workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a prospect or customer">
    Add a third party record with the company name, type set to at least **Prospect**, and contact details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log an opportunity">
    On the third party, open the **Opportunities** tab and create an opportunity with the expected amount and close date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a commercial proposal">
    From the opportunity (or directly from the menu), create a proposal. Add product lines, generate a PDF, and send it with the online signing link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customer signs">
    The customer clicks the signing link. The proposal moves to **Signed** status and a notification is sent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Convert to order">
    Click **Convert to order** on the signed proposal. Set the warehouse for stock reservation and validate the order.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create shipment (if applicable)">
    Generate a shipment from the order, pick the goods, and validate the shipment to update stock.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the invoice">
    From the order, click **Create invoice**, then validate it. The invoice is now posted to the accounting journal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invoice">
    Click **Send by email** to deliver the invoice PDF to the customer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record payment">
    When payment arrives, open the invoice and click **Add payment**. Select the bank account and confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reconcile (optional)">
    In the **Banking** module, match the recorded payment to your bank statement line to complete the reconciliation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Discounts and rebates

- **Absolute discount per customer**: set a fixed monetary credit on the third-party record (e.g. from a credit note credit). Applied during invoice creation.
- **Percentage discount**: enter a discount percentage on each proposal or invoice line, or apply a global discount at the document level.
- **Price levels**: configure multiple price lists (e.g. wholesale, retail) and assign a price level to each customer.

## PDF generation

Dolibarr ships with several PDF templates for each document type. Switch between them at any time:

1. On the document, scroll to the **PDF** section.
2. Select a template from the dropdown (e.g. `crabe`, `standard`, `einstein`).
3. Click **Generate** to produce or refresh the PDF.

Custom PDF templates can be created using PHP or ODT format and placed in the appropriate template directory.

## Point of Sale (TakePOS)

For retail and walk-in sales, enable the **TakePOS** module. TakePOS provides a touch-optimised interface for scanning products, applying discounts, and recording cash or card payments. All TakePOS transactions create standard Dolibarr invoices and update stock in real time.
