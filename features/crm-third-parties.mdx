In Dolibarr, almost every business relationship flows through the **Third Parties** module. A third party is a single unified record that can simultaneously be a customer, a prospect, a supplier, or any combination — eliminating duplicate records and giving you a 360-degree view of every organisation you interact with.

## What is a third party?

Dolibarr uses the term "third party" (tiers in French) to describe any external entity your organisation deals with. A single record can carry one or more of these roles:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Customer" icon="building">
    An organisation or individual who buys goods or services from you. Customer invoices and orders are linked to this record.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Prospect" icon="binoculars">
    A potential customer in your pipeline. Prospects can receive commercial proposals and be converted to customers once a deal is won.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Supplier" icon="truck">
    An organisation you purchase from. Supplier invoices and purchase orders are linked here.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
A third party can hold all three roles at the same time. For example, a partner who both buys from you and sells to you only needs one record.
</Note>

## Creating a third party

Navigate to **Third Parties → New Third Party** to open the creation form.

### Required fields

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Name** | The company or individual's full name. Used across all linked documents. |
| **Type** | Customer, Prospect, Supplier, or any combination. Sets which modules link to this record. |

### Optional profile fields

- **Alias / Short name** — a shorter display name for internal use
- **Address, ZIP, Town, Country** — used on generated PDFs (invoices, proposals)
- **Phone / Fax / Email / Website** — displayed on the record and available for mass emailing
- **Staff count and turnover** — useful for segmentation and scoring

### Country and VAT settings

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="VAT number">
    Enter the EU VAT registration number in the **VAT ID** field. Dolibarr can validate European VAT numbers using the built-in VIES check under **Third Parties → Check VAT number**.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Country">
    Selecting the correct country applies the right VAT rules automatically. For intra-EU sales, setting the country to an EU member state triggers reverse-charge behaviour when the customer provides a valid VAT number.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Local taxes">
    For jurisdictions with local taxes (e.g. Spanish RE/IRPF), additional tax fields appear once the appropriate country is selected and the local-tax module is enabled.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Contacts

Contacts are people linked to a third party. A single company record can have many contacts, each with their own role, phone number, and email.

- Go to the **Contacts** tab on a third party record to add or view contacts.
- A contact can be linked to **multiple third parties** (for example, a consultant who works across several of your clients).
- Contacts can be granted **portal access** so they can view their own invoices and proposals online.

<Tip>
Use the **Job title** field on a contact to filter later when deciding who to include in a mass-emailing campaign.
</Tip>

## CRM: opportunities and pipeline

Enable the **CRM / Leads** module (**Setup → Modules**) to unlock the full opportunity pipeline.

### Pipeline stages

```text
Prospect → Lead → Opportunity → Won / Lost
```

Each stage maps to a configurable probability percentage. You can customise stage labels and percentages at **Setup → CRM → Opportunity statuses**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create an opportunity">
    Open a third party and click the **Opportunities** tab, then **New opportunity**. Set the stage, expected close date, and estimated amount.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign a sales owner">
    Set the **Sales representative** field to track which team member owns the deal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link a commercial proposal">
    Once you are ready to send a formal offer, create a **Commercial Proposal** directly from the opportunity. The proposal inherits the third-party details automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close the deal">
    Mark the opportunity **Won** or **Lost**. Won opportunities can trigger automatic conversion of the linked proposal to a customer order.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Logging activities

All interactions with a third party are recorded through the **Agenda / Events** module.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Logging a phone call">
    From any third-party record, click **Add event** and select the event type **Phone call**. Set the date, duration, and a short note. The entry appears on the third party's **Agenda** tab and on your personal calendar.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Scheduling a meeting">
    Create a new event of type **Meeting**, assign one or more participants (internal users or external contacts), and set a start/end time. Participants receive an email notification if configured.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Logging an email sent">
    Emails sent directly from Dolibarr (proposals, invoices) are automatically logged. You can also add a manual **Email** event to record conversations that happened outside the system.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Linking events to tasks or projects">
    Every event can be linked to a project and task, allowing time spent with a customer to feed directly into project time tracking.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Categories and segmentation

Assign **categories** to third parties to group and filter them for targeted campaigns or reporting.

- Create category labels at **Setup → Categories → Third parties**.
- Assign one or more categories to each third party from its **Categories** tab.
- Filter the third-party list by category using the search panel on the left side of the list view.

<Tip>
Combine categories with the **Mass emailing** module to send newsletters or announcements to a specific segment without exporting a list first.
</Tip>

## Import and export

### Bulk import via CSV

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download the template">
    Go to **Tools → Import** and choose the **Third parties** data object. Download the CSV template to see the expected column headers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Populate the file">
    Fill in name, type, address, country code, VAT number, and any custom fields. Keep country codes as ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 (e.g. `FR`, `DE`, `GB`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload and map">
    Upload your file, then use the column-mapping screen to match your CSV headers to Dolibarr fields. You can save the mapping as a profile to reuse it later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the import">
    Review the preview rows, then click **Import**. Dolibarr reports any rows that fail validation without stopping the rest of the import.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Export

Use **Tools → Export** and select the **Third parties** object to export filtered records to CSV or Excel.

## Searching and filtering the third-party list

The list view at **Third Parties → List** has a collapsible filter panel on the left:

- Filter by **type** (customer / prospect / supplier)
- Filter by **sales representative**
- Filter by **category**, **country**, or **activity status**
- Use the top search bar for a full-text search across name, alias, email, and phone

<Warning>
The **Active / Inactive** filter is separate from the type filter. An inactive third party is hidden from autocomplete fields by default but still accessible through the list. Set a third party to inactive when a business relationship ends rather than deleting the record, to preserve historical documents.
</Warning>
