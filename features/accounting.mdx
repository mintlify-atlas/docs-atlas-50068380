---
title: "Accounting and financial management in Dolibarr"
description: "Manage bank accounts, reconcile transactions, run SEPA credit transfers, and produce accounting journals and tax reports with Dolibarr's accounting module."
---

Dolibarr integrates financial management directly with its operational modules: every validated invoice posts to an accounting journal, every recorded payment updates a bank account, and every bank statement line can be reconciled against those entries — all without leaving the application.

## Bank accounts

Navigate to **Banking → Bank accounts** to manage all your financial accounts.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a bank account">
    Click **New bank account**. Enter the account label, bank name, account number (IBAN for European accounts), BIC/SWIFT, and currency. Set the **type** to Bank, Cash, or Credit card to determine how it appears in reports.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record a manual transaction">
    Open the account and click **Add transaction**. Enter the date, description, amount (positive for credit, negative for debit), and an optional reference. Use this for transactions not generated by Dolibarr (bank charges, transfers between accounts, etc.).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Import a bank statement">
    Click **Import transactions** to upload a file in OFX, QIF, or CSV format. Dolibarr parses each line and presents them for matching against existing payments.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Bank reconciliation

Reconciliation ensures that payments recorded in Dolibarr match your actual bank statement.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the account">
    Navigate to the bank account and click **Reconcile**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the statement date and closing balance">
    Enter the statement end date and the closing balance from your bank statement. Dolibarr uses this to calculate the expected reconciling difference.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tick off matching lines">
    Each unreconciled transaction is listed. Check the box next to each line that appears on your statement. The running balance updates as you tick lines.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm reconciliation">
    When the difference reaches zero, click **Validate reconciliation**. Matched lines are marked as reconciled and no longer appear in future reconciliation sessions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Import your bank statement file before reconciling. This gives you statement lines to compare against Dolibarr entries, making it far easier to spot discrepancies.
</Tip>

## Accounting module

Enable the full double-entry accounting module at **Setup → Modules → Accountancy**.

### Chart of accounts

Dolibarr ships with localised charts of accounts for France (PCG), Belgium, Spain, and other countries. Go to **Accounting → Chart of accounts** to:

- Browse and edit account numbers and labels.
- Add custom accounts.
- Assign default accounting accounts to products, VAT rates, and third parties.

### Accounting journals

| Journal | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Sales journal** | Entries from validated customer invoices and credit notes. |
| **Purchase journal** | Entries from validated supplier invoices. |
| **Cash/Bank journal** | Entries from recorded payments and bank transactions. |
| **General/Operations journal** | Manual journal entries and miscellaneous adjustments. |
| **Expense reports journal** | Entries from validated employee expense reports. |

Navigate to **Accounting → Journals** and select a journal to see, filter, and export its entries.

### Posting invoices

When you validate a customer or supplier invoice, Dolibarr automatically generates the corresponding accounting entry using the accounts configured on the product lines, VAT rates, and third party. No manual journal entry is needed for routine invoicing.

<Note>
If the accounts are not configured on a product or VAT rate, Dolibarr cannot generate an automatic entry and will report a binding error. Configure default accounts at **Accounting → Setup → Default accounts**.
</Note>

## SEPA payments

Dolibarr supports the two main SEPA file formats for European electronic payments.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="SEPA Direct Debit (prélèvement)">
    Use this to collect payments from customers who have signed a mandate authorising you to debit their account.

    1. Record the customer's IBAN and BIC on their third-party record under **Payment methods**.
    2. Record a signed mandate reference and signature date.
    3. Go to **Banking → Direct debit orders → New order**. Select the invoices to collect.
    4. Generate the SEPA XML file (pain.008) and upload it to your bank.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="SEPA Credit Transfer">
    Use this to pay suppliers and employees via bank transfer.

    1. Record the supplier's IBAN and BIC on their third-party record.
    2. Go to **Banking → Credit transfer orders → New order**. Select the supplier invoices to pay.
    3. Generate the SEPA XML file (pain.001) and upload it to your bank.
    4. Once the bank confirms execution, mark the payments as sent to reconcile the invoices.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## VAT and tax reports

<Steps>
  <Step title="Configure VAT rates">
    Go to **Setup → Dictionaries → VAT rates** to define the tax rates applicable in your country. Assign rates to products and services as defaults; these can be overridden on individual document lines.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate a VAT report">
    Navigate to **Accounting → Taxes → VAT report**. Select the period (month or quarter). Dolibarr calculates VAT collected (output tax) and VAT paid on purchases (input tax) and shows the net amount due to the tax authority.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export for filing">
    Download the report as PDF or CSV for submission to your tax authority, or use the country-specific DGI/DEB/ESL exports available in some localisation modules.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
VAT reports are based on **validated** invoices only. Invoices left in draft state are excluded, which may cause discrepancies if you validate invoices retroactively.
</Warning>

## Fiscal year management

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define fiscal years">
    Go to **Accounting → Fiscal years**. Create a record for each fiscal year with its start and end date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close the fiscal year">
    At year end, run **Accounting → Year-end closing**. Dolibarr generates the closing and opening journal entries that carry the balance forward to the next year.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate reports">
    Use **Accounting → Reporting** to produce a trial balance, income statement, or balance sheet for the closed period.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common accounting workflows

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Posting a customer invoice to accounting">
    1. Create and validate a customer invoice (the entry is generated automatically).
    2. Go to **Accounting → Journals → Sales journal** to confirm the entry was created.
    3. If the entry shows a binding error, check that the product lines and VAT rate have accounting accounts assigned.
    4. Use **Accounting → Bind invoices** to manually bind any unbound entries.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Recording and posting a payment">
    1. Open the validated invoice and click **Add payment**.
    2. Enter the amount, date, payment method, and bank account.
    3. Validate. Dolibarr creates a bank journal entry and closes the invoice if fully paid.
    4. The bank account balance updates immediately.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Reconciling the bank account">
    1. Import your bank statement (OFX/QIF/CSV) into the bank account.
    2. Open **Reconcile** and set the statement date and closing balance.
    3. Match Dolibarr transactions to statement lines by ticking the corresponding rows.
    4. When the difference is zero, confirm reconciliation. Matched lines are locked.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Running the VAT report">
    1. Go to **Accounting → Taxes → VAT report**.
    2. Set the period start and end date.
    3. Review output VAT (collected from customers) and input VAT (paid to suppliers).
    4. Download the PDF and submit to your tax authority, or use the e-filing export if available for your country.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Margins

Enable **Margins** at **Setup → Modules → Margins** to track profitability.

- Margins are calculated as sell price minus buy price for each product line.
- View margin per invoice, per customer order, or per product in the margin reports at **Billing → Margins**.
- The margin percentage and absolute margin appear on the document line detail when the module is active.

## Multi-currency

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Setup">
    Enable multi-currency at **Setup → Modules → Multi-currency**. Add currencies and set exchange rates at **Setup → Currencies**. Rates can be updated manually or fetched automatically from a currency API.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Transactions">
    Invoices and payments in a foreign currency show amounts in both the document currency and your base (accounting) currency. The exchange rate at the document date is recorded and stored on the document for audit purposes.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Accounting entries">
    All accounting journal entries are posted in the base currency using the exchange rate on the document. Foreign-currency unrealised gains and losses must be handled through manual journal entries at period end.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Donations and loans

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Donations" icon="heart">
    Enable the **Donations** module to record charitable contributions. Each donation generates a receipt PDF and an accounting entry. Useful for non-profits and foundations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Loans" icon="landmark">
    Enable the **Loan** module to track long-term borrowings. Record the principal, interest rate, and repayment schedule. Dolibarr calculates amortisation and generates the repayment journal entries.
  </Card>
